SURFER

Surfer Magazine stands as a cornerstone in the world of surfing, tracing its roots back to its founding in 1960 by John Severson, a pioneering figure in the sport. Severson, a passionate surfer and talented artist, recognized the need for a publication that would not only document the burgeoning surf scene but also capture the essence of the lifestyle. With this vision in mind, he launched Surfer Magazine, which quickly became the definitive voice of surfing culture. Severson's blend of captivating photography, insightful journalism, and artistic flair laid the foundation for a publication that would shape the trajectory of surfing for generations to come.

Surfer Magazine's significance to the surfing culture cannot be overstated. Beyond merely chronicling the sport, it has served as a conduit for surfers to share their stories, experiences, and aspirations. Through its pages, surfers from all walks of life have found a sense of belonging and connection to the oceanic world they love. Whether it's showcasing the latest innovations in board design, profiling legendary surfers, or highlighting the most coveted surf destinations, Surfer Magazine has been a source of inspiration and education for surfers around the globe.

Moreover, Surfer Magazine has played a pivotal role in shaping the identity of surfing as a lifestyle. It has transcended its role as a mere publication to become a cultural institution, influencing fashion, music, art, and even language. The magazine's imagery and ethos have permeated popular culture, helping to define what it means to be a surfer. Through its unwavering commitment to authenticity and passion for the sport, Surfer Magazine continues to be a cherished symbol of the surfing community's spirit, resilience, and sense of adventure.

This website serves as an archive of some of the best covers throughout the incredibly influential and culturally powerful life of Surfer Magazine.
